Blood Donation Eligibility

Ensure you meet the health and safety criteria before donating blood.

Basic Requirements

  • Age: 18 – 65 years (some centers accept 16–17 with parental consent).
  • Weight: At least 50 kg (110 lbs).
  • Hemoglobin level: Within normal range.
  • Good general health and feeling well on the donation day.

Medical Conditions That May Delay Donation

  • Fever, flu, or cold within the past 7 days.
  • Recent surgery (within 6 months).
  • Pregnancy or breastfeeding (defer for at least 6 months after delivery).
  • Recent blood donation (must wait at least 3 months before donating again).

Lifestyle & Travel Considerations

  • No history of drug use via injection.
  • Must not have been exposed to HIV or other blood-borne infections.
  • Travel to malaria-endemic areas may require a temporary deferral.

Why Eligibility Matters

Blood donation safety protects both the donor and the recipient. Ensuring eligibility helps maintain a clean blood supply, prevents adverse reactions, and ensures that every donation truly saves lives.
